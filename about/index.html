About Me

Dear friends, welcome to my personoal webpage! My name is Rui Luo (罗睿), I was born in Moyang Town (沫阳镇), Luodian County, China. My hometown is Dongdang (董当乡), which is a small but beautiful village, about ten miles away from the Five-hundred meter Aperture Spherical Telescope (FAST). I grew up in Luodian (罗甸县), a county located in South Guizhou, China.

<img src='./hometown.jpg' align='center' style='width:600px; height:450px' / alt='Dongdang'> <center>(A view of Dongdang)</center>

Now I am a PhD candidate at Department of Astronomy (DoA) and Kavli Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics (KIAA), <img src='./test_backend.JPG' align='right' style='float; width:300px; height:220px' alt='Testing FRB backend' /> Peking University. My current research projects concentrate on a new astronomical phenomenon called Fast Radio Burst (FRB), including theoretical and observational aspects. My PhD thesis focuses on FRB statistical studies. Meanwhile, I'm a member of FRB searching team based on PKU-XAO-YNAO collaboration.

My hobbies cover different aspects widely. For sports, I like football(soccer), swimming, badminton, table tennis, e-sports, etc. Besides, I love hiking and reading, I believe either my body or soul should be on the way after all. During my free time, I also enjoy listening to light music and think about the Universe, who we are, why we here, and where we go ...
